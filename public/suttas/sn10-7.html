sn10.7:0.1 Linked Discourses 10.7
sn10.7:0.2 1. With Indaka
sn10.7:0.3 With Punabbasu

sn10.7:1.1 At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.
sn10.7:1.2 Now at that time the Buddha was educating, encouraging, firing up, and inspiring the mendicants with a Dhamma talk about extinguishment.
sn10.7:1.3 And those mendicants were paying attention, applying the mind, concentrating wholeheartedly, and actively listening.
sn10.7:1.4 Then the native spirit Punabbasu’s Mother soothed her little children, saying:

sn10.7:2.1 “Hush, little Uttarā!
sn10.7:2.2 Hush, Punabbasu!
sn10.7:2.3 For I want to listen to the teaching
sn10.7:2.4 of the Teacher, the supreme Buddha.

sn10.7:3.1 Since the Blessed One spoke of extinguishment,
sn10.7:3.2 the release from all ties,
sn10.7:3.3 I have a lasting love
sn10.7:3.4 for this teaching.

sn10.7:4.1 In this world, your own child is dear;
sn10.7:4.2 in this world, your own husband is dear;
sn10.7:4.3 but even greater than that is my love
sn10.7:4.4 for this teaching’s quest.

sn10.7:5.1 For neither son nor husband,
sn10.7:5.2 dear as they are, can free you from suffering;
sn10.7:5.3 as listening to the true teaching
sn10.7:5.4 frees living creatures from suffering.

sn10.7:6.1 In this world mired in suffering,
sn10.7:6.2 fettered by old age and death,
sn10.7:6.3 I want to listen to the teaching
sn10.7:6.4 that the Buddha awakened to,
sn10.7:6.5 which frees you from old age and death.
sn10.7:6.6 So hush, Punabbasu!”

sn10.7:7.1 “Mom, I’m not speaking,
sn10.7:7.2 and Uttarā is silent, too.
sn10.7:7.3 Focus just on the teaching,
sn10.7:7.4 for it’s nice to listen to the true teaching.
sn10.7:7.5 And it’s because we haven’t understood the teaching
sn10.7:7.6 that we live in suffering, Mom.

sn10.7:8.1 He is a beacon for those who are lost
sn10.7:8.2 among gods and humans.
sn10.7:8.3 The Buddha, bearing his final body,
sn10.7:8.4 the Clear-eyed One teaches Dhamma.”

sn10.7:9.1 “It’s good that my child’s so astute,
sn10.7:9.2 this child I bore and suckled!
sn10.7:9.3 My child loves the pure teaching
sn10.7:9.4 of the supreme Buddha.

sn10.7:10.1 Punabbasu, may you be happy!
sn10.7:10.2 Today, I rise.
sn10.7:10.3 Hear me too, Uttarā:
sn10.7:10.4 I have seen the noble truths!”
