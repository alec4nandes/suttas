Aṅguttaranikāya — Numbered Discourses Collection — (AN)

The “Numbered” or “Numerical” Discourses are usually known as Aṅguttara Nikāya in Pali, abbreviated AN. However, the Pali tradition also knows the form Ekottara (“one-up” or “incremental”), and this is the form usually found in the northern collections. These collections organize texts in numbered sets, from one to eleven. Compared to the other nikāyas, they are more oriented to the lay community. The Ekottarikāgama (EA) in Chinese is a highly unusual text, which features a range of variations within itself when it comes even to basic doctrines. It shares considerably less in common with the Pali Aṅguttara than the other collections do with their counterparts. In addition, there is a partial Ekottarikāgama in Chinese, as well as a variety of individual discourses and fragments in Chinese and Sanskrit.

Dutiyapaṇṇāsaka — The Second Fifty

Bhūmicālavagga — The Chapter on Earthquakes — (AN 8.61–70)

Alaṁsutta — (AN 8.62)

Eight sets of qualities pertain to benefiting oneself and others.

an8.62:0.1 Numbered Discourses 8.62
an8.62:0.2 7. Earthquakes
an8.62:0.3 Good Enough

an8.62:1.1 “Mendicants, a mendicant with six qualities is good enough for themselves and others.
an8.62:1.2 What six?
an8.62:1.3 A mendicant is quick-witted when it comes to skillful teachings.
an8.62:1.4 They readily memorize the teachings they’ve heard.
an8.62:1.5 They examine the meaning of teachings they’ve memorized.
an8.62:1.6 Understanding the meaning and the teaching, they practice accordingly.
an8.62:1.7 They’re a good speaker. Their voice is polished, clear, articulate, and expresses the meaning.
an8.62:1.8 They educate, encourage, fire up, and inspire their spiritual companions.
an8.62:1.9 A mendicant with these six qualities is good enough for themselves and others.
