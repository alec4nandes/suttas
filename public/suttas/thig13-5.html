thig13.5:0.2 Chapter of the Twenties
thig13.5:0.3 Subhā, the Daughter of a Blacksmith

thig13.5:1.1 “I was young and pure
thig13.5:1.2 when I heard the Dhamma.
thig13.5:1.3 I understood the truth
thig13.5:1.4 through heedfulness,

thig13.5:2.1 and then all sensual pleasures
thig13.5:2.2 stopped being so appealing.
thig13.5:2.3 I saw the danger in self-identity
thig13.5:2.4 and longed for renunciation.

thig13.5:3.1 I left behind relatives,
thig13.5:3.2 workers and servants,
thig13.5:3.3 and prosperous village fields,
thig13.5:3.4 pleasing and delightful.

thig13.5:4.1 I gave up much wealth
thig13.5:4.2 for the sake of going forth,
thig13.5:4.3 Now that I have left the household life
thig13.5:4.4 out of faith in the well-spoken true Dhamma,

thig13.5:5.1 it wouldn’t be proper
thig13.5:5.2 for one who aspires to nothingness,
thig13.5:5.3 to revert to gold and silver
thig13.5:5.4 after casting them away.

thig13.5:6.1 Silver or gold
thig13.5:6.2 do not bring enlightenment nor peace.
thig13.5:6.3 They are not proper for a contemplative,
thig13.5:6.4 this isn’t noble wealth.

thig13.5:7.1 It’s just greed and intoxication,
thig13.5:7.2 delusion and increase of impurity.
thig13.5:7.3 It is unreliable, troublesome,
thig13.5:7.4 and short-lived.

thig13.5:8.1 Human beings with defiled minds pursue it.
thig13.5:8.2 Heedless and lustful,
thig13.5:8.3 they get into numerous quarrels,
thig13.5:8.4 pitting themselves against each other.

thig13.5:9.1 One can see those entangled in sensual pleasures
thig13.5:9.2 afflicted with much misfortune:
thig13.5:9.3 ruin, imprisonment, hardship,
thig13.5:9.4 loss, grief, and lamentation.

thig13.5:10.1 Treacherous relatives,
thig13.5:10.2 why do you try to trap me in sensual pleasures?
thig13.5:10.3 You know I have gone forth
thig13.5:10.4 after seeing the danger in sensual pleasures.

thig13.5:11.1 Neither gold nor money
thig13.5:11.2 will eliminate the influences of mind.
thig13.5:11.3 Sensual pleasures are murderous enemies,
thig13.5:11.4 foes, darts, fetters.

thig13.5:12.1 Treacherous relatives,
thig13.5:12.2 why do you try to trap me in sensual pleasures?
thig13.5:12.3 You know I have gone forth
thig13.5:12.4 with a shaven head, wrapped in the outer robe.

thig13.5:13.1 Collecting lumps of leftover food,
thig13.5:13.2 and robes made from discarded rags:
thig13.5:13.3 these are the supports for the homeless life,
thig13.5:13.4 this is what is proper for me.

thig13.5:14.1 The great sages have spewed out sensual pleasures,
thig13.5:14.2 both divine and human.
thig13.5:14.3 They are in a safe place, they are liberated,
thig13.5:14.4 they have reached unshakable happiness.

thig13.5:15.1 I should not get involved with sensual pleasures,
thig13.5:15.2 where safety is not found.
thig13.5:15.3 Sensual pleasures are deadly enemies,
thig13.5:15.4 they are like a great mass of fire, they are suffering.

thig13.5:16.1 Greed is hazardous, frightful,
thig13.5:16.2 pestering, troublesome.
thig13.5:16.3 It is the opposite of peace,
thig13.5:16.4 and the way into much delusion.

thig13.5:17.1 Sensual pleasures are like the head of a snake,
thig13.5:17.2 dangerous and dire.
thig13.5:17.3 Foolish blind worldlings
thig13.5:17.4 delight in them—

thig13.5:18.1 indeed there are many foolish beings in the world,
thig13.5:18.2 trapped in the mud of sensual pleasures,
thig13.5:18.3 who do not know the end
thig13.5:18.4 of birth and death.

thig13.5:19.1 Because of sensual pleasures
thig13.5:19.2 humans are on the path that leads to a bad destination.
thig13.5:19.3 Many decide to follow it,
thig13.5:19.4 bringing illness to themselves,

thig13.5:20.1 by producing enmity,
thig13.5:20.2 torment, and complete defilement.
thig13.5:20.3 Sensual pleasures are entrapping,
thig13.5:20.4 the world’s bait that binds us to death.

thig13.5:21.1 Maddening, enticing,
thig13.5:21.2 sensual pleasures crush the mind.
thig13.5:21.3 They are a trap set by Māra
thig13.5:21.4 for the defilement of sentient beings.

thig13.5:22.1 Sensual pleasures have endless drawbacks,
thig13.5:22.2 cause much suffering, and are highly poisonous.
thig13.5:22.3 They bring little enjoyment, they produce conflict,
thig13.5:22.4 causing one’s bright wholesome traits to wither away.

thig13.5:23.1 I will not go back
thig13.5:23.2 to sensual pleasures,
thig13.5:23.3 which have caused me so much misery—
thig13.5:23.4 now I always enjoy Nirvana.

thig13.5:24.1 When I was battling with sensual pleasures,
thig13.5:24.2 I longed for a state of coolness—
thig13.5:24.3 now I dwell heedful
thig13.5:24.4 in the destruction of all fetters.

thig13.5:25.1 Free from sorrow, stainless, safe:
thig13.5:25.2 I follow the upright
thig13.5:25.3 Noble Eightfold Path,
thig13.5:25.4 the one because of which the Great Sage crossed over.”

thig13.5:26.1 “You see this person established in Dhamma,
thig13.5:26.2 Subhā, the daughter of a blacksmith.
thig13.5:26.3 Having become free from passion,
thig13.5:26.4 she meditates at the root of a tree.

thig13.5:27.1 Today is the eighth day since her going forth,
thig13.5:27.2 faith shining in the true Dhamma.
thig13.5:27.3 Trained by Uppalavaṇṇā,
thig13.5:27.4 possessing the three knowledges, leaving behind death.

thig13.5:28.1 A free person with no debt,
thig13.5:28.2 a bhikkhunī with well-developed spiritual powers,
thig13.5:28.3 with all bonds unbound,
thig13.5:28.4 she is one who has done what was to be done, and is free from influences.”

thig13.5:29.1 Then Sakka, using psychic powers,
thig13.5:29.2 approached her with an assembly of Devas,
thig13.5:29.3 and the lord of beings paid homage to
thig13.5:29.4 Subhā, the daughter of a blacksmith.

thig13.5:30.1 … The Elder Subhā, daughter of a blacksmith.

thig13.5:31.1 The Chapter of the Twenties is finished.
